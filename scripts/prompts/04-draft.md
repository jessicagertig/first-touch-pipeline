## Ground the voice in her real emails
Read ALL 88 records in the library. Study HOW Jessica writes — patterns, not rules:
- She names the company and reacts to ONE specific, concrete thing she actually noticed — not their high-level value prop, and never the mundane core function.
- Plain, everyday words — her OWN description, never the company's marketing slogans/buzzwords. If their site says "embedded integration platform" or "zero-emission fleet," she would not repeat that phrasing.
- Never flowery or inflated. Often blunt and warm: "looks great," "looks like a great company."
- No strategy verdicts. Avoid clichés like "forward-looking move," "a thoughtful reimagining of what X can be."
- A light first-person touch ("I love…") is optional — MANY of her compliments have no "I/me" at all. Do NOT force a personal reaction.
- Don't preface with reaction-framing ("what stood out to me," "stuck with me," "keep coming back to") — just state the thing.
- ALWAYS name the company in the compliment, leading with it (e.g. "FrontierU looks great – …"). Never compliment them without naming the company.
